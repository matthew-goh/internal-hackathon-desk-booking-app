# Office Booking — Known Problems

## Core Issues (Team-Identified)

- **Booking on behalf of others** — bookings made without the actual user's involvement, creating accountability gaps
- **Forgetting to unbook** — reservations left active after plans change, blocking space unnecessarily
- **Not honouring bookings** — people ignore existing reservations and use spaces without checking

## Ghost Bookings & Space Waste

- Desks and rooms reserved "just in case" and never used
- No consequence or friction for no-shows, so the behaviour repeats
- System shows office as full; office is actually half-empty

## Visibility & Coordination

- No way to know who's coming in before you arrive — people show up hoping to sit near their team
- Peak-day clustering (typically Tue–Thu) causes overcrowding while Mon/Fri sit empty
- Last-minute visitors can't find space because ghost bookings consume availability

## Space Misuse

- Pods and lounge used as overflow desk space rather than their intended purpose
- No guidance or enforcement on what each space type is for
- Quiet spaces undermined by loud calls or group work

## Booking Experience

- No desk profiles — users don't know which desks have monitors, standing options, or power nearby
- Poor real-time availability information leads to double bookings or wasted search time

## Management & Planning

- Office managers can't predict attendance — heating, catering, and resourcing decisions made on gut feel
- No utilisation data to identify patterns (peak days, underused rooms, chronic no-shows)
- Impossible to plan capacity or justify space costs without reliable occupancy data
